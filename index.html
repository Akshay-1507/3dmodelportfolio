Project 1
Train Collision Avoidance Using Piezo Sensors
A train collision avoidance system using piezoelectric sensors detects obstructions like rocks on railway tracks, preventing accidents. Piezo sensors placed along the tracks sense vibrations or pressure changes caused by falling objects. A microcontroller processes these signals, determining if there's a threat. Upon detection, the system sends alerts to railway authorities or directly triggers train brakes. This solution is ideal for hilly, landslide-prone areas, ensuring safer rail travel by preventing collisions through early detection and automated responses.
Train Collision Avoidance Using Piezo Sensors
The train collision avoidance system utilizes a combination of sensors, data processing, and communication mechanisms to detect potential obstructions on railway tracks and prevent accidents. Piezoelectric sensors are installed along the tracks to monitor vibrations or pressure changes caused by falling rocks or debris. A microcontroller processes the signals from these sensors, analyzing the data to determine if there is a significant threat while filtering out irrelevant disturbances. Upon detection, the system sends alerts to railway control centers and can directly interface with the train’s onboard systems to trigger automatic braking or reduce speed. The use of multiple sensors enhances reliability, ensuring continuous monitoring even if one sensor fails. This comprehensive approach enables real-time tracking of track conditions, significantly improving railway safety by preventing collisions and ensuring passenger security.
